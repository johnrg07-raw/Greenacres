Our Story

A homestead built with love, one animal at a time.

Green Acres began in 2015, hatching and raising healthy chicks from our free-range flock. What started as a modest family project has grown into a fifteen-acre working farm — and a small community of happy, well-loved animals with names, personalities, and stories worth telling.

We're not a petting zoo. We're a real homestead, and our tours reflect that. When you visit, you're stepping into our home. You'll meet each animal one by one, learn what makes them unique, and leave knowing a little more about where your food, fiber, and farming traditions come from.

— The Kweller Family

The Farm Tour

An unhurried, hands-on afternoon on a real working farm.

Our tours are private, educational, and unhurried. You won't be shuffled through a queue — you'll be walked through our home, animal by animal, with stories, facts, and plenty of time for questions (and selfies).

  • 01

    Private & Personal

    By appointment only — so it's just you, your group, and our family. No crowds, no rushing.

  • 02

    Step-by-Step Education

    We introduce each animal one by one, sharing care, diet, and the story of how they came to live here.

  • 03

    Feed & Connect

    Feed the goats, hold a chicken, meet the pigs — you're part of the chores for an afternoon.

  • 04

    Great for Kids & Grown-ups

    Birthdays, school groups, date days — whoever's coming, they're leaving with a story.

Good to Know

A few house rules that keep our animals happy and our tours magical.

  • Appointment only. No walk-ins, please.
  • Closed-toe shoes. Barn floors aren't sandal-friendly.
  • We are not a petting zoo. We're a real working homestead.
  • Tours run roughly 60–90 minutes. Bring water on hot days.
  • Photos welcome — just be gentle with the animals.
